## Micro Journal Rev.3: Nadia

Micro Journal Rev.3: Nadia is a small keyboard that connects easily to a phone or tablet. It is meant for moments on a sofa. It works well even when laying down. The cable can be tucked away into a small pocket when your writing session is completed. 

I wanted to make a keyboard that looks good while keeping the phone holder naturally attached to it. Functional, yet feeling like a coherent part of the design. When the middle panel flips, it holds the phone. A natural transition that guides the user to see the keyboard together with the phone. 

Sometimes I wonder if anyone really wants to write on a phone. Yet from time to time I find myself in that exact situation. Sitting on a sofa, the phone in my hand, needing to write something longer than a few words. It usually begins with a simple reply to an email. Or a long comment on social media that refuses to stay short. The screen keyboard appears. My thumbs begin their small dance across the glass. Slowly the message forms. It always works. 

Still, in those moments I often feel the same small wish returning. I want a keyboard that can rest on my lap or across my thigh. Something stable enough to stay with me through the whole thought. Something that lets the words arrive without fighting the screen. I wish I had this keyboard.

### Switch Types

#### **Clicky**
  * Noticeable click and tactile bump sound.
  * Clear feedback when the key activates.
  * Preferred by writers and typists who enjoy feeling each keystroke.
  * Crisp and responsive typing sensation.

#### **Silent**
  * Very quiet — ideal for shared or quiet environments.
  * Smooth, gliding, and buttery key feel.
  * Reduced finger fatigue during long typing sessions thanks to smooth travel.
